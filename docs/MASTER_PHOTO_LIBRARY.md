## Editorial Consistency Standard

All 52 images must share a cohesive visual language. Before sourcing, internalize these rules:

**Colour temperature:** Warm. Every image should lean toward amber, ochre, golden, or earth tones. Avoid cool blue-cast images unless they are intentional pre-dawn/night shots that suit the dark sections of the site.

**Processing style:** Natural and unmanipulated. No HDR halos, no heavy vignetting, no oversaturation. The Serengeti grass should look like dry honey, not electric green.

**Composition ethic:** Patient, earned, unhurried. Not trophy shots or peak-action at all costs. The image should feel like a moment witnessed, not a performance captured.

**Subject dignity:** Animals at rest or in natural behaviour. People photographed with respect and agency — never staged "tribal" shots. Guides and rangers depicted as professionals, not props.

**Technical floor:** Every image must be sharp at 100% at the minimum resolution specified. Slight natural grain is acceptable. Noise artefacts, motion blur on the subject, and chromatic aberration are not.
